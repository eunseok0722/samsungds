Privacy Policy (For DS Brand Desk Website)

Samsung Electronics Co., Ltd. (“Company”) values your personal information and strives to comply with the “Korean Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection, etc.” and the “Korean Personal Information Protection Act”.

In this Privacy Policy, we explain how we use your personal information and what measures are taken to safeguard the privacy of the data.

0. In General

“Personal Information” refers to any information relating to a living individual that makes it possible to identify the individual by his/her full name, resident registration number, image, etc. (including information which, if not by itself, makes it possible to identify any specific individual if combined with other information).

This Policy is available on the main page of DS Brand Desk website (http://semiconductor.samsung.com/branddesk) where you can access it at any time.

We have implemented all the necessary procedures in order to update the Privacy Policy for its continuous improvement. If we update this Privacy Policy, we let you know about any changes by adding a version number to it.

This Privacy Policy comes into effect on 13 July, 2022. We will inform you of any updates by placing a notice on the website (or in writing, via e-mail, phone, or SMS).

1. Users and Parents’ Rights and How to Excise Them

(1) At any time, a user and the holder of parental responsibility can request access, rectification, deletion, restriction of processing, or withdrawal of consent in relation to the personal information of the user or the person under the age of 14 concerning the following matters:

– What personal information we have:
– The use or provision of personal information to third parties: or
– The status of the user’s consent to the collection/use/provision of personal information:

Such request can be made by using the “Change Personal Information” (or “Change Membership Information”) menu on the homepage, calling 1588-4730, or contacting the Chief Privacy Officer in writing or via phone or e-mail. We may refuse your request to access, rectify, or delete all or some of your information in the following cases:

– Access to the information is prohibited or restricted by law; or
– There is a risk of a threat of death or bodily harm, or a risk of unfair infringement of another person’s property and other interests.

(2) If you wish for us to correct inaccuracies in your information, we will not use or provide your information to a third party until the correction is completed. In addition, if incorrect information has already been provided to a third party, we will amend it with the correct details immediately.

Personal information that has already been deleted or the processing of which has been restricted upon your request is processed in line with the section referring to the “7. Retention and Use Period” and cannot be accessed or used for another purpose.

You are required to enter your up-to-date information. You are responsible for any incidents caused by the provision of inaccurate information. If you provide false information, such as another person’s details, you may lose your membership.

When it comes to the privacy of personal information, you have the right be protected, as well as the obligation to exercise caution and not infringe others’ information. Please be careful that your personal information, such as passwords is not leaked, and ensure that you do not damage the personal information of others, such as posts. If you fail to fulfill this responsibility and damage others’ information and dignity, you may be subject to punishment under the “Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection, etc.”.

3. Installation, Operation, and Refusal of Automatic Personal Information Collection Tools

(1) Purpose of Cookies

Cookies serve many purposes, including: automatic login feature; analysis of access frequency or visit time by members and non-members; finding out users’ preferences and interests; keeping track of users; or providing targeted advertising and customization services based on users’ participation in various promotions and the number of website visits.

(2) How to Change Your Cookie Preferences

You have the right to choose not to accept cookies. You can configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is sent. However, disabling cookies may affect your ability to use some services or certain parts of them.

– How to change your cookie settings (if you are using Internet Explorer 11.0) : Go to the “Tools” menu and select “Internet Options”. Click on the “Privacy” tab. Select the “Advanced” button and amend these options as you wish.
– How to view stored cookies (if you are using Internet Explorer 11.0) : Go to the “Tools” menu and select “Internet Options”. Click on the “General” tab and then on the “Browsing history”. Select “Settings” and “View Files” and then amend these options as you wish.
– How to refuse cookies (if you are using Internet Explorer 11.0) Go to the “Tools” menu and select “Internet Options”. Click on the “Privacy” tab. Select “Default” and move the slider to the top to “Block all cookies”.

8. Destruction Procedures and Methods

Procedures and methods for the destruction of personal information are as follows:

(1) Destruction Procedures

After its purpose is fulfilled, users’ personal information is moved to a separate DB (in the case of printed material, this should be stored in paper cabinets) and is kept for a certain period and is destroyed for privacy protection in accordance with the internal policy and applicable law (see the “7. Retention and Use Period”). The personal information kept in a separate DB is not used for another purpose, unless otherwise required by law.

(2) Destruction Methods

All personal information stored in electronic format is deleted by using technology that prevents the data from being restored. Personal information printed out on paper is shredded or incinerated.

9. Outsourcing Personal Information Processing and International Transfers

We outsource the processing of personal information to third parties in order to provide services. Personal information processing is outsourced to each service provider, only to the extent that this will be necessary to provide each service individually.
In respect of personal information disclosed to service providers, in order to safeguard privacy we mandate such service providers clearly to: comply with data protection instructions; keep personal information confidential; not share data with third parties; take full responsibility in the event of an incident; and return or destroy personal information after outsourcing or use of the data ceases. 10. Others

(1) Technical and Organizational Measures to Safeguard Personal Information

We have implemented the following technical and organizational measures to prevent personal information from being lost, stolen, leaked, altered, or damaged in the course of processing users’ personal information.

– Internal Management Plan
ㆍWe have established and implemented an internal management plan for ensuring the secure processing of personal information.
ㆍWe have set up an division dedicated to privacy and data protection, which checks whether the concerned personnel comply with the safeguards for personal information and promptly take corrective measures upon detecting a problem.

– Access Control
ㆍWe control unauthorized access through an intrusion prevention system and seek to equip all technical devices in order to ensure security in the system.

– Prevention against Access Record Forgery and Alteration
ㆍWe keep and manage the access records of the personal information processing system and use the security features in order to prevent the access records from being forged or altered.

– Encryption
ㆍYour personal information is protected by passwords, stored, and managed after encrypting the files and transmitted data or locking the files. Important data is protected using additional security features.

– Prevention against Cyber Attacks
ㆍWe use antivirus software to prevent any cyber threat. Our antivirus program is regularly updated. In case of a new threat, we provide the antivirus solution the moment it is available in order to prevent personal data breaches.
ㆍWe adopt Secure Socket Layer (SSL), which ensures the secure transmission of personal information via the network by using an encryption algorithm.
ㆍWe utilize intrusion prevention systems and vulnerability assessment systems on each server in order to prevent cyberattacks.
ㆍWe do not keep personal information and general data together but store them separately using individual servers.

– Minimization and Education of Staff Who Handle User Information
ㆍWe grant access to users’ personal information only to those who are involved in marketing activities dealing with customers directly, those who manage personal information, such as the Chief Privacy Officer and other relevant personnel, and those who inevitably handle personal information for business purposes.
ㆍWe provide regular education programs for employees who handle personal information both within the company and through outside professionals about new security technology and personal information protection obligations.
ㆍWe prevent human-caused data breaches by making all employees sign a confidentiality pledge before joining the company and putting in place internal procedures to audit compliance with the personal information protection measures by employees.
ㆍThe transfer of duties to successors from those who handle personal information is thoroughly conducted under high security, and we hold employees for any data incident responsible after joining or leaving the company.
ㆍWe designate computer rooms and data archives as restricted areas and control the access to them.

(2) Third Party Links

We may provide links to third-party websites, products, and services. Certain products or services developed by third parties may be available through our services (e.g., apps developed by third parties available through Samsung Galaxy Apps). In this case, because third-party websites, materials, products, and services are outside our control, we are not responsible for and cannot guarantee their usefulness. When you move to another website by clicking a link featured on our services, products, websites, and apps, the website’s privacy practices are independent from our own policy. Therefore, we encourage you to learn about their privacy practices.

(3) Posts

We take your posts seriously and endeavor to protect them against alteration, damage, or deletion, with the following exceptions:

– Spam posts;
– Defamatory posts containing false information intended to defame others;
– Posts revealing others’ identity without consent;
– Posts infringing others’ rights, such as intellectual property rights of Samsung or a third party; or
– Posts irrelevant to the topic of a certain board.

In order to promote a desirable culture in post boards, we may delete or modify certain sections in your post if an individual's identity is disclosed without consent. If the content of your post is better suited for another board with a different topic, we will move it to the board and reveal the path to its new location in order to avoid misunderstanding.

Besides the above cases, other disqualified posts may be deleted after an explicit or separate warning.

Basically, as an author you have all rights and responsibilities related to your posts. It is difficult to protect voluntarily disclosed information, therefore please ensure that you take this aspect into full consideration prior to publication.

(4) Prohibition of Unauthorized Collection of E-mail Addresses

We will never collect e-mail addresses without permission or by using an e-mail address collector program or other software. In case of violation, we may be subject to penalties under the Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection, etc.

(5) Direct Marketing Communications

We do not send marketing messages for commercial purposes without your prior consent.
